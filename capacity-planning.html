How it works

Three inputs you already have.

01

Your work, weighted by certainty

Awarded jobs count in full. Pipeline pursuits are discounted by win probability and document confidence — an invite-only tease doesn't move your plan the way a full, reviewed package does.

02

Weekly demand by role vs your bench

Labor templates turn each job into a week-by-week crew curve — installers, foremen, PMs, support — so you see exactly where you're short and where you're carrying idle bench.

03

Hire / hold / bridge — gated by margin

Every recommendation is checked against the money: a hire only fires when gross margin covers the fully-burdened cost. Awarded-but-underfunded gaps say "bridge with outside labor," not "add payroll."

Why it's different

Not a scheduling board. Not an accounting tool. A capacity decision layer for subs.

Built only for subcontractors

Workforce tools are built for general contractors managing crews across projects. This starts from a sub's reality: bid invites, incomplete documents, and the decision of what to pursue.

Starts before the award, not after the schedule

Capacity is a pre-bid input, not a post-award scheduling chore. It answers "should we chase this given who we'd need?" while there's still time to decide.

Models the money, not just headcount

A full chart doesn't mean you can afford it. Capacity puts gross margin against your fixed and semi-fixed labor so a profitable project never quietly creates a company-level labor problem.

Your numbers stay yours

Wages, margins, and pipeline live in your private account, enforced by row-level security. We never sell your data or share it with GCs.
