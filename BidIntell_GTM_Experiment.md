# BidIntell — GTM Demand Experiment (run this before building anything else)

**Date:** 2026-06-02
**Owner:** Ryan Elder
**Status:** ACTIVE — kill/go decision after 10 hand-scored bids
**Context:** 8 weeks post-launch, 0 paying customers. Beta users "liked it" but were "too busy to mess with it." The job now is demand discovery, not product.

---

## The bet we're testing

Subcontractors waste real time/money chasing bad bids, and a personalized GO/REVIEW/PASS score is worth enough that they'll pull it into their workflow.

**The signal that put this in doubt:** "Everyone likes the idea but everybody's too busy to use it." That sentence means one of two things, and we don't yet know which:

- **Case A — Not painful enough.** Subs have decided bids on gut for decades. If true, this is a vitamin. No GTM saves a vitamin.
- **Case B — Real pain, too much activation friction.** The 12-step onboarding + upload + weight config is more work than the bad bid it prevents. "Too busy" = setup isn't worth it. Fixable.

**This experiment is designed to tell A from B in ~2 weeks, for free, building nothing.**

## Why hand-scoring is the right instrument

When *you* score their bid and hand them the report, activation friction goes to **zero** — they do nothing. That strips out the onboarding confound. So:
- If they still don't care → **Case A**. The premise is wrong. Learn it now.
- If they pull on you for more → **Case B**. Pain is real; the product job becomes "make the first report instant, zero setup" (the diagnostic-agent model, productized).

## Protocol

1. **List 10 subs you know by name.** Warm only. Not the dream-150 cold list — that's parked until this converts.
2. **Call, don't email.** Script:
   > "I built a tool that scores whether a bid's worth chasing — GO, REVIEW, or PASS, with the why. Send me your next real invite and I'll score it and send you a one-page report. Takes me five minutes, costs you nothing."
3. **Score their real, live bid by hand.** Use the app yourself; deliver the one-page report (the diagnostic-agent output).
4. **Watch what they DO, not what they say.** Get on a screen-share or sit with them when they open it if you can.
5. **Log the outcome the same day** (table below).

## What counts as a signal (behavior, not politeness)

Only these count as a YES:
- [ ] Asks you to score their **next** bid
- [ ] **Changes their bid decision** because of the report
- [ ] Asks **"how do I get this myself?"**
- [ ] **Refers** someone ("you should show this to ___")

"Cool, thanks" + silence = **NO**. Log it as a no. Do not round up to "interested."

## Kill / Go thresholds

- **3+ pulls out of 10 → GO.** Demand is real. Next job is NOT more features — it's killing activation friction so the first report takes 60 seconds with zero setup. Then turn the hand-scored subs into paying users.
- **1–2 pulls → WEAK.** Something's there but the wedge is wrong. Re-interview the pullers: what specifically made them pull? Narrow to that.
- **0–1 pulls → STOP and rethink the premise.** It's a vitamin as framed. Cheaper to learn it now than after another quarter of building. Go back to office-hours / problem discovery.

## The BuildingConnected question (resolve with data, not the one comment)

One beta user said "I'd use it if it connected to BuildingConnected." That's either the real wedge (meet bids where subs already live) or a polite brush-off. The "where do they get bid invites" column answers it:
- If most warm subs live in BC and the BC guy **pulls** after a hand-scored report → integration is the wedge worth building.
- If the BC guy still doesn't act on a free hand-scored report → BC was an excuse, not the blocker. Don't build it.

## Guardrails (what NOT to do during this experiment)

- **Park the dream-150 LinkedIn outreach.** Zero warm conversions so far; cold strangers convert worse than warm ones. Wide outreach is for after this proves the thing pulls.
- **Don't build features.** Not the 5-dimension scoring upgrade, not Phase 3 anything. The moat is theoretical until you have users logging outcomes. Users first.
- **Don't count "likes the idea" as progress.** Only the four pull signals count.

## Timebox

2 weeks. Report the pull count. That number is the company's pulse.
