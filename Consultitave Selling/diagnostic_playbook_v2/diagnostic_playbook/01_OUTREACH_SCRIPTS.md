# 01 — OUTREACH SCRIPTS

**Purpose:** Get a 30-minute diagnostic call on the calendar.
**Channels:** Warm email, cold-warm email (referrals), LinkedIn DM.
**Voice:** Confidently Boring. No exclamation points. No AI hype. Plain estimator language.

---

## RULE ZERO

Every outreach must do three things:

1. Establish that you are *not* asking them to try a product
2. Frame the ask as research / peer conversation
3. Promise a written deliverable they get whether they convert or not

If a draft you write skips any of these three, rewrite it.

---

## SCRIPT 1 — WARM NETWORK EMAIL (THE GHOSTERS)

**Use for:** Prior beta testers, prospects who said yes then went silent, LinkedIn 1st-degree contacts you've actually met.

**Subject:** Different ask — 30 minutes on how you pick bids

> Hey [Name],
>
> Coming back to you with something different than last time. Not asking you to try anything.
>
> I'm doing research on how specialty sub owners actually decide what to bid. Not the textbook answer — the real version. Which GCs you'll never chase again, which ones you wish you'd skipped, what the gut-feel pattern looks like when you're staring at a bid invite Tuesday morning.
>
> I'd love 30 minutes to walk through your last 10 bids with you — wins, losses, the ones you regret chasing. I'll write up what I see and send you a one-page read on your bid selection patterns. Stuff I doubt anyone has ever sat down and shown you.
>
> No pitch attached. Just trying to learn what's actually happening in shops like yours, and I think you'll get something useful out of seeing it written down.
>
> Worth a half hour next week?
>
> Ryan

**Variant for prospects who specifically said "I'll try the trial" and didn't:**

Replace paragraph 1 with:

> Hey [Name],
>
> Last we talked you mentioned you'd give the BidIntell trial a try. No worries that life got in the way — that's pretty much everyone. Coming back to you with something different.

The acknowledgment matters. You're letting them off the hook for ghosting, which removes the social cost of replying.

## SCRIPT 3 — LINKEDIN DM (WARM)

Shorter, less formal, fits LinkedIn pace. **Use this format for any LinkedIn message — never longer.**

> Hey [Name] — different angle than last time. Not pitching anything.
>
> Doing research on how sub owners actually pick what to bid (the real version, not the LinkedIn version). Looking at win/loss patterns across ~30 shops your size.
>
> Would you walk me through your last 10 bids? 30 min, I'll send you a written read on your selection patterns. No pitch.

**Why this works on LinkedIn:** Mobile-readable. No preamble. Makes the ask in 4 lines. The "(not the LinkedIn version)" parenthetical lands with construction guys because they already think LinkedIn is mostly noise.

## SCRIPT 5 — TEXT MESSAGE (WARMEST CONTACTS ONLY)

For people whose cell number you have and whose relationship is strong enough to warrant the channel.

> Hey [Name] — Ryan. No pressure to try anything. Different ask than last time. Doing research on how subs actually pick bids — would you sit with me 30 min and walk through your last 10? I'll write up what I see and send it back. No pitch attached.

**Critical:** Only text people you'd text about a kid's birthday party or a Chiefs game. If the relationship doesn't warrant texting personally, use LinkedIn or email.

## RESPONSE HANDLING

### "Yes, let's do it"

Reply within 24 hours:

> Great. Here's my Calendly: [bidintell.ai/diagnostic-call or direct Calendly link]
>
> Pick anything that works. There's a short intake form on the booking page — takes 60 seconds, just helps me show up prepared. Talk soon.

### "What's this for again?" / "Tell me more"

> Sure. Short version: I built BidIntell because I lived this problem on the sub side for years and didn't have the tools I needed. Before I keep building, I'm sitting down with 30 specialty sub owners to make sure the way I think about bid selection actually matches how you do.
>
> The call is 30 minutes. I ask 5 structured questions about your last 10 bids — wins, losses, regrets, your decision process. After the call I write you a 1-page memo on what I see in your selection patterns. You keep that whether anything else happens or not.
>
> No pitch on the call. Worth a half hour?

### "I'm too busy"

> Totally get it. The standing offer is open whenever — if a quieter week shows up in the next month or two, just reply and we'll grab time. No follow-up pressure from me in the meantime.

**Do not push.** Mark them as "deferred" in the tracking sheet. Try again in 60 days with a different hook.

### "Send me more info first"

> Happy to. Honest answer though — there isn't really more info to send beyond what I described. The diagnostic is the conversation. The deliverable is the memo. If 30 minutes feels like too much to commit without seeing more, that's a reasonable instinct, but I don't have a brochure for this. The whole thing IS the brochure.
>
> Want to grab a 15-minute intro call instead? I can walk you through what the 30-min looks like and you can decide from there.

**This response weeds out tire-kickers and gets you to a yes-or-no faster.**

### "Is this a sales call?"

> Fair question. The 30 minutes is research, not sales. I will not pitch BidIntell on the call. Period.
>
> What I will do, separately, about a week after I send you the memo: send you a short follow-up email that mentions BidIntell because that's where this research feeds. You can reply or ignore. The memo and the call are yours either way.
>
> Still worth half an hour?

**Honesty wins here. Don't oversell the "no sales" angle — there is a follow-up. Be straight about it.**

### "Can you just send me a sample memo?"

> I can't share another shop's memo — confidentiality matters and they trusted me with their bid data. But I'd rather just run yours. Worth half an hour to find out?

If they push: don't relent on this. Sharing a real memo (anonymized) might seem like a shortcut, but it kills the scarcity of the deliverable being *theirs*.

## CADENCE & VOLUME

**Week 1-2:** Send 10 warm-network emails (Script 1). Send 5 LinkedIn DMs (Script 3). Aim for 5 calls booked.

**Week 3-4:** Add 10 cold-warm emails (Script 2). Continue LinkedIn DMs. Aim for 5 more calls booked.

**Week 5+:** Inbound from LinkedIn posts and (eventually) the /diagnostic page should be carrying 30-50% of bookings.

**Hard ceiling:** 5 diagnostics per week. If you book more, you starve the build/sales/admin side of the business. Better to under-book than over-book.
