## POST 1 — THE OPENER

**Post this Monday or Tuesday of week 1.**

```
Most specialty subs lose money on the bids they shouldn't have chased — not the ones they lost.

Spent 20 years in commercial construction. 10 on the GC side, then 10 building a Division 10/12 specialty sub from zero to about $8M before I shut it down when it stopped being profitable.

The bids that hurt weren't the close losses. They were the ones we knew on day 3 we weren't going to win, but we'd already burned the estimating time.

I'm running free 30-minute bid selection diagnostics for 10 specialty sub owners ($2M–$20M revenue) this month.

What you get: walk through your last 10 bids with me, then a written one-page read on your selection patterns. Specific GCs and scopes I'd flag based on your data.

What I get: pattern data across the trade. I'm building software in this space and the only way to build it right is talking to the people doing the work.

No pitch attached. I'll confirm that in writing.

Comment "diagnostic" or DM me if you want one of the slots.
```

**Notes:**
- The "shut it down when it stopped being profitable" line is the credibility hook. Don't soften it. Sub owners respect that more than success stories.
- Keep "Comment 'diagnostic'" — easier than asking them to DM, and the public comment is social proof for everyone else watching.

## POST 2 — THE OBSERVATION

**Post 7 days after Post 1, AFTER you've completed at least 1-2 diagnostics.**

If you haven't completed any yet, hold this post and use Post 3 instead until you have data to reference.

```
Started the diagnostic conversations last week. A pattern showed up that I half-expected but is starker than I thought.

Almost every sub I've talked to so far can name the GC they should stop bidding. They know which one. They know the win rate is 1 in 8 over the last two years. They know the contracts are punishing.

They're still bidding them.

Not because they want to. Because the invite hits, the estimator opens it, and three hours later the takeoff is half-done and the inertia carries it the rest of the way. There's no decision moment built into the workflow. The bid happens because nobody stopped it.

Most subs don't have a bid/no-bid problem. They have a bid-by-default problem.

A few diagnostic slots still open this month. 30 minutes, walk through your last 10 bids, get a written read on your selection patterns. Free. No pitch on the call.

DM me or comment "diagnostic."
```

**Notes:**
- "1 in 8 over the last two years" is a specific number — only use it if your diagnostic data actually shows this. If your real number is different, swap it. Specific numbers = credibility, made-up specifics = damage.
- The reframe "not a bid/no-bid problem, a bid-by-default problem" is the post's keeper line. People will quote it back to you.

The actual decision — should we spend 8 hours of estimating time on this invite — almost never gets made deliberately. It just happens.

A few slots left. DM me.

**Notes:**

## POST 4 — THE CASE STUDY

**Post AFTER you've completed 5+ diagnostics. The specifics need to be real (anonymized) for this to land.**

Template — fill in with real data from your diagnostic dataset:

```
Quick case from the diagnostic conversations.

Owner of a [TRADE] sub, about $[X]M revenue. Walked through his last 10 bids with him. Wins were spread across [N] GCs he'd worked with for years. Losses were almost all from one GC he'd never won with — 0 for 7 in 18 months.

Asked him why he kept bidding them. He said: "[VERBATIM QUOTE FROM YOUR ACTUAL CALL]"

But he won't get it. The 0 for 7 isn't variance. It's a signal. Probably a stalking horse situation, probably a competitor with a relationship he can't break into.

Cost him about [X] hours of estimating time over those 7 bids. At his estimator's rate, that's real money for a $[X]M shop.

If you're an owner of a $2M–$20M specialty sub and you want a written read on what your last 10 bids actually say about how you're picking work, comment "diagnostic" or DM me. Free 30-minute call. No pitch.

A few slots open this month.
```

**Anonymization rules:**
- Trade can be specific (flooring, insulation, MEP) — many shops fit each category
- Revenue rounded to nearest $5M
- GC name NEVER used — always "one GC"
- Verbatim quote allowed if it doesn't identify the person — generic quotes ("the work would be good if we got it") are fine, specific ones ("we've been chasing the Sprint tower for three years") are not

## ENGAGEMENT HANDLING

**When someone comments "diagnostic":**

Public reply within 4 hours (same day at minimum):
```
Sent you a DM.
```

Then DM:
```
Hey [first name] — Ryan. Thanks for the comment.

Calendly: [your URL]

Pick anything that works. Short intake form on the booking page — takes 60 seconds. Talk soon.
```

**When someone DMs you directly without commenting:**

Reply within 4 hours:
```
Hey [first name] — Ryan. Thanks for reaching out.

Calendly: [your URL]

Pick a time. Quick intake form on the booking page. Looking forward to it.
```

**When someone reacts/likes but doesn't comment or DM:**

Don't initiate. They saw it, they didn't act. If they're 1st-degree and ICP-fit, you can DM with a softer version 2-3 weeks later — but only if they're a real fit, not just any liker.

**When someone comments with a question instead of "diagnostic":**

Answer the question publicly (fast, helpful, no pitch). The audience values the answer more than the post sometimes. Then DM them privately if they're ICP-fit:
```
Saw your comment on the diagnostic post — wanted to follow up directly. If the format sounds useful, [Calendly link]. No pressure either way, happy to keep talking in comments.
```

## DON'T DO THIS

- Don't post about the diagnostic more than once a week
- Don't repost prior diagnostic posts verbatim ("here's a slot opening up")
- Don't tag specific GCs, even positively
- Don't tag specific competitors
- Don't post about specific prospects, even anonymously, without their permission
- Don't quote from a memo you sent without explicit permission to use the quote publicly
- Don't add exclamation points to "make it pop"
- Don't add emojis "for engagement"

These rules exist because your edge in this space is *credibility*, not entertainment. You're talking to estimators, not consumers. They scroll past flashy and stop on direct.
