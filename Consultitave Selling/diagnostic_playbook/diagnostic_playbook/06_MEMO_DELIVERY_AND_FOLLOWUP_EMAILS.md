# 06 — MEMO DELIVERY EMAIL

**Purpose:** Send the memo with framing that lands the value, sets expectations for follow-up, and doesn't pitch.
**Timing:** 3-5 business days after the call.

---

## THE EMAIL — STANDARD TEMPLATE

**Subject:** Your bid selection memo — [Company Name]

> Hey [Name],
>
> Memo attached. One page covering the patterns I saw across the 10 bids we walked through.
>
> Three things you'll find in it:
>
> The shape of your bid selection — how I'd describe your current process based on what you told me. Tell me if I got it wrong.
>
> Three patterns I noticed — including [light teaser of the most striking pattern, e.g. "the [GC name] situation we talked about"]. The pattern section is the part most people print and put on their desk.
>
> Two suggestions — concrete things you could try in the next 60 days. No software required, no buy-in from anybody else. Stuff you could do with index cards if you wanted to.
>
> Few caveats:
>
> This is one outside read based on a 30-minute conversation. You know your business better than I do — if any of this lands sideways, that's signal worth me knowing.
>
> The memo is yours. Share it, save it, throw it out. No strings.
>
> I'll follow up next week with one short email — that's the BidIntell mention I told you about on the call. After that, the ball's in your court.
>
> Thanks again for the time, [Name].
>
> Ryan
>
> Ryan Elder
> Founder, BidIntell
> ryan@bidintell.ai

---

## VARIANT — 5-7 PAGE REPORT VERSION

If you sent the longer report instead of the 1-pager, swap the second paragraph:

> Memo attached. Seven pages this time — your call had enough texture to warrant the longer write-up. Don't feel obligated to read it cover to cover. The GC-by-GC section on page 4 is probably the highest-leverage piece for your situation.

---

## VARIANT — IF THE PROSPECT WAS GUARDED ON THE CALL

If the prospect gave brief answers and the memo is necessarily lighter, set expectations:

> Memo attached. Shorter than usual — your call was disciplined and we kept it tight, so the memo follows suit. The patterns are still real, just fewer of them than I sometimes find.

This frames brevity as a feature, not a flaw.

---

## DO NOT INCLUDE IN THIS EMAIL

- Any link to bidintell.ai
- Any mention of pricing
- Any mention of features
- Any "want to see how BidIntell handles this?" line
- Any urgency or scarcity
- Any "Calendly link to chat" CTA

The memo email is delivery, not conversion. Conversion comes in the follow-up.

---

## REPLY HANDLING

### "This is great, thanks"

Reply within 24 hours, but keep it short:

> Glad it landed. Talk next week.

That's it. Don't pitch. Don't push.

### "Some of this isn't quite right — [correction]"

GREAT response. Reply:

> Appreciate the correction — that's exactly the kind of feedback I want. I'll update my notes. The follow-up next week will reflect the corrected read.
>
> Ryan

Then update the tracking sheet with the correction. The fact they engaged enough to push back means high conversion potential.

### "What's next?"

If they ask this, the follow-up plan accelerates:

> Next is up to you, honestly. Two paths:
>
> 1. I send the follow-up next week as planned — short email mentioning BidIntell and offering to score one of your real bid invites for you. No commitment. You decide if it's interesting.
>
> 2. We just do that now. You send me a bid invite you've gotten this week, I run it through BidIntell, send you the analysis. Same thing, just sooner.
>
> Either works. What's your preference?

If they pick option 2, you've compressed the timeline by a week. Move fast.

### "I need to think about it" / no reply within a week

Send the standard follow-up email (file 08) on day 7-8. Don't escalate or guilt-trip.

### "This made me realize my whole process is broken"

This is the deepest possible response. Don't pitch — just be a peer:

> Yeah. That feeling is more common than you'd think. The good news is that "I have no real process" is a much easier starting point than "I have a bad process I'm attached to."
>
> Follow-up email next week as planned. Talk soon.
>
> Ryan

The follow-up email after that response will land hard because they've already done the emotional work of recognizing the gap.

# 08 — FOLLOW-UP EMAIL (THE BRIDGE TO BIDINTELL)

**Purpose:** Convert the diagnostic relationship into a BidIntell conversation.
**Timing:** 5-7 days after memo sent. Not sooner.
**This is the conversion email. It needs to be tight.**

---

## STRUCTURE OF A GOOD FOLLOW-UP

A great follow-up has 4 elements in this order:

1. **Specific reference back to the memo** — the most striking pattern from THEIR call
2. **The natural bridge to BidIntell** — why what they told you maps to what BidIntell does
3. **A tiny ask** — send one bid invite, you'll score it, no signup
4. **A graceful exit** — explicit "if not, no problem"

Length: 7-10 lines max. Mobile-readable.

---

## THE EMAIL — STANDARD TEMPLATE

**Subject:** That [pattern from memo] — quick follow-up

> [Name] —
>
> Been thinking about [the specific pattern from the memo, named in their language]. The thing that struck me was [one specific quote or detail from the call].
>
> I built BidIntell partly to handle exactly this — score the GC fit, flag contract risks, and surface the location and trade match before you commit estimating time.
>
> Quick test: send me one bid invite you've gotten in the last week. I'll run it through BidIntell and send you the BidIndex score plus the contract risk read on it. No login, no trial signup. Just the output on your real work.
>
> If it's useful, we can talk about what a paid version looks like. If not, you've got the memo and we move on.
>
> Worth a try?
>
> Ryan

## RESPONSE HANDLING

### They send a bid invite

Drop everything. Score it within 24 hours. This is your highest-conversion-probability moment.

Reply with the BidIntell analysis as a PDF + 3-5 sentences of context:

> [Name] —
>
> Bid analysis attached. Quick read:
>
> BidIndex score: [X]/100
> Top contract risks: [list 2-3]
> What I'd flag: [1-2 sentence editorial — your peer take, not the software's]
>
> Want to set up a 7-day trial so you can run more of these yourself? It's free, no card required to start.
>
> Ryan

### They reply "send me a trial link"

> Here you go: [bidintell.ai/signup or direct trial link]
>
> Use code FOUNDER30 for 30% off for life if you decide to convert.
>
> One quick thing: best way to get value out of the trial is to upload 3-5 real bid invites in the first 48 hours. Want me to walk you through your first one on a 15-minute screen share?

### They reply "I'll think about it"

> Sounds good. I'll leave the door open. If you want to send a bid invite in the next month or so, the offer stands.
>
> Ryan

Then mark in the tracking sheet as "warm — re-engage in 60 days."

### No reply

Wait 14 days, then send ONE more nudge:

**Subject:** Last note from me

> [Name] — last note from me on this thread, then I'll stop bugging you.
>
> Standing offer: send me a bid invite, I'll score it, no signup. Open whenever.
>
> If now's not the time, no problem. The memo is yours regardless.
>
> Ryan

After this, **stop**. No more follow-ups. Mark as "deferred" in tracking sheet. Try again in 90 days only with a genuine new hook (product update, new pattern from research, etc.).

---

## CONVERSION RATES TO EXPECT

Realistic benchmarks based on this motion:

- 80%+ of prospects open the memo email
- 40-60% reply to the memo email (any reply, including "thanks")
- 30-50% reply to the follow-up email
- 20-30% send a bid invite or start a trial
- 10-15% convert to paid

So roughly: **for every 10 diagnostics, expect 1-2 paid conversions** in the first 60 days.

If you're below those numbers after 10 calls, the issue is in one of three places:
- ICP fit (you're booking calls with people who can't say yes)
- Memo quality (the deliverable isn't landing)
- Follow-up timing or copy (the bridge isn't working)

Diagnose which one and fix it. Don't generalize.
