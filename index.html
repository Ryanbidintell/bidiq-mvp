07How scoring works

What the BidIndex Score actually measures

The BidIndex is a fit score — not a win probability. It's a triage tool: spend time on bids worth chasing, skip the ones that aren't.

You set the weights

The percentages below are default weights — not hardcoded rules.

In Settings → Scoring, every contractor tunes how much each factor counts toward their BidIndex. Push weight to what matters for your shop; the four must add up to 100. That's why the same bid scores differently for two companies.

Relationship-First Risk-Averse Trade Specialist Market-Volume
Default weighting ◆ adjustable per account
30%
Location Fit
default · you set yours

Distance from your office to the site vs. the service radius you set. Closer scores higher — crew mobilization, supervision, and daily logistics favor work near base. If location doesn't matter for you, its weight redistributes automatically.

Project city/state Drive distance Service radius
25%
Contract & Scope
default · you set yours

Matches scope language against your good/bad keywords, plus contract risk detection. Clause patterns like pay-if-paid, unlimited lien waivers, and compressed schedules are flagged and can apply a penalty based on your risk tolerance.

Good keywords Risk keywords Clause detection
25%
Client Relationship
default · you set yours

Looks up the named client against your Clients list. Uses your actual win rate if you've worked together, your star rating if you've rated them, or a default for unknowns. Your history is private to your account.

GC / client match Star rating Win rate
20%
Trade Match
default · you set yours

Checks whether the scope actually includes your trade, via CSI MasterFormat section codes — reading spec headers, drawing sheet prefixes (M-, P-, E-, FP-), material keywords, and invitation scope. Section-level matching (09 65 00) is far more precise than division-level.

CSI codes Sheet prefixes Material keywords
